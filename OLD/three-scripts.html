Three Scripts

Type Design Hebrew Typography Editorial Book Design Speculative Design Award Winning
Three Scripts is an award-winning typographic project exploring three new Hebrew writing systems. It investigates the "word" through three distinct lenses: as a magical force, a primordial concept, or a digital code. This study pushes the boundaries of how meaning and form coexist within the Hebrew language.
Each conceptual pillar translates into a unique design logic. One system is mathematically structured via gematria values; another draws from the raw, organic roots of Proto-Canaanite script; and the third is a rigid, modern construction based on the structural constraints of Unicode.
The project is presented in three phases: the Book of Explanations documents the research and foundations; the Specimen Booklet showcases the aesthetic development of the typefaces; and the Book of Designs demonstrates their application in contemporary visual contexts.
The project was honored with the AlefAlefAlef Design Award for 2025 graduates, recognizing excellence in Hebrew typography. It is also preserved in the Madaf.art archive, a digital project dedicated to the curation and preservation of local Israeli design.
