בקשו את חילזון
Gemtaria of the Tzitzit is a visual and structural study of the ancient art of tying ritual fringes. The project explores the intersection of sacred tradition and typographic form, transforming a physical act of religious observance into a systematic visual language rooted in Jewish mysticism and heritage.
Focusing specifically on the Sephardi tying tradition, the research highlights how the number of loops in each knot corresponds to Hebrew letters through Gematria. This realization—that the knot is already a carrier of linguistic data—served as the foundation for a new typographic system.
The physical structure of the knots was translated into a comprehensive graphic alphabet from Alef to Tav. Using a precise diagrammatic approach, each character was designed to maintain a direct visual and mathematical link to the original ritual ties, bridging the gap between fiber and font.
To demonstrate the system's utility, the new alphabet was applied to create "encoded ornamentations." These complex, ornate frames function as both decorative elements and hidden texts, where the traditional aesthetic of Jewish manuscripts is reimagined through modern, systematic design logic.
