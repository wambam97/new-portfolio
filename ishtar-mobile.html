01.The Story

[ 0 ]

ISHTAR is a Jerusalem-founded fashion house built on the visual identities of the Middle East. By translating regional traditions into contemporary design, the brand serves as a platform for Mizrahi art—preserving heritage through strict craftsmanship and sustainable form.

[ CHAPTER 02 ]

02.Symbols

[ 0 ]

Rooted in regional visual traditions, the logo integrates the hamsa with a fluid, grapevine-inspired motif. The final identity distills this fusion into a stark, fragmented geometric icon, balancing historical symbolism with contemporary edge.

02.1.Brand Seal

[ 0 ]

The seal preserves the symbol's organic origins. While the primary logo distills the hamsa and grapevine motif into a sharp contemporary glyph, the seal retains the fluid, vegetal qualities of the original drawing. Used as a secondary emblem, it serves as a mark of origin, reinforcing the brand's connection to craft, place, and heritage.

02.2.Logotype

[ 0 ]

The logotype draws inspiration from the structural strokes of ancient Babylonian cuneiform writing

[ 0 ]

The characters of the Ishtar logotype were meticulously integrated into the abstracted symbol, merging language and icon

02.2.Fluidity

[ 0 ]

The symbol is designed to morph and adapt to any dimension, reinforcing a versatile and fluid brand identity that resonates with everyone, everywhere

03.Typography &Palette

[ a ]

Helvetica provides the foundational structure for the Ishtar visual identity. Chosen for its neutrality and exceptional legibility, this sans-serif typeface ensures that primary messaging remains clear and accessible.
